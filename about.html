About Me

Who I am

Hi, I’m Patrick—an Indonesian‑American Computer Science student at Lamar University who enjoys building things that are both useful and delightful to use. I learn fastest by making projects, which is why I split my time between coursework, small apps, and game prototypes. I’m comfortable moving between the design of an interface and the logic behind it, and I care a lot about clarity and kindness in the way software behaves.

My current interests sit at the intersection of web development, systems, and data. I like the challenge of translating ambiguous ideas into concrete, testable solutions, and I try to write code that future‑me (and future teammates) can understand.

Strengths

People I work with tend to describe me as steady, curious, and reliable. I ask good questions, I document what I learn, and I’m not afraid to open a terminal and dig into logs when something feels off. I enjoy helping others unblock, and I’m proactive about improving small details—naming, errors, and automations—that make a team faster.

Technical toolkit

Daily drivers: Python, C/C++, JavaScript, HTML/CSS, SQL, and Linux. I’m also comfortable with Git/GitHub, shell scripting, Excel/VBA, and basic networking. Recently I’ve been experimenting with Unity and Lua scripting (Roblox Studio) to learn game loops, state, and simple networking concepts.

What I’m learning now

This year I’m deepening my foundations with courses like Cybersecurity: Systems, Distributed Systems, Introduction to Computer Architecture, Operating Systems, UNIX++, and Database Design. These classes sharpen my understanding of how software works under the hood—processes and threads, memory, filesystems, synchronization, query planning, and secure design principles.

Values

I value respect for users, clear writing, small composable pieces over big rewrites, and practicality over hype. I try to create interfaces that feel welcoming, and back them with dependable code and good logs.

Outside class

When I’m not coding, I like exploring coffee spots around Beaumont/Houston, weight training, and tinkering with personal finance spreadsheets. I also enjoy mentoring friends through intro CS topics and interview prep.
